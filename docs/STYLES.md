# STYLES

## Intent

- The app should feel like a personal finance cockpit: calm, polished, and a little premium.
- Pages should help users scan quickly, spot changes, and move into detail views without friction.
- Visual tone should balance warmth and clarity instead of feeling purely corporate or purely playful.

## Core look

- Use light surfaces with depth from gradients, soft shadows, blur, and layered panels.
- Keep the main text palette in slate or near-black for readability.
- Use warm amber and fresh teal as accent colors for energy and contrast.
- Avoid flat white-on-white layouts when a page is meant to feel important or summary-driven.
- Prefer rounded corners in the `rounded-2xl` to `rounded-3xl` range for major cards and sections.

## Layout

- Favor roomy dashboard-style composition with a strong top section and compact supporting panels below.
- Use grids to create clear information hierarchy rather than long vertical stacks.
- Keep sections modular so pages can be rearranged without rewriting large components.
- Let the page breathe with visible spacing between major blocks.

## Components

- Primary cards should feel substantial: border, soft shadow, clean padding, clear title, and one main takeaway.
- Hero sections can use gradients, blur, and contrast panels to create a stronger first impression.
- KPI cards should be compact, scannable, and visually consistent.
- Quick-link areas should feel actionable, not like plain lists.
- Charts should sit inside polished containers and support the surrounding story instead of dominating it.

## Typography

- Use concise headlines with strong hierarchy and tight tracking for key summary areas.
- Support headlines with short explanatory copy instead of long paragraphs.
- Use uppercase micro-labels sparingly for metadata, section labels, and small badges.
- Keep dense data readable with small text only where the information is secondary.

## Color and emphasis

- Use dark slate for the strongest anchors and summary values.
- Use amber for warmth, highlights, and moments that suggest momentum or attention.
- Use teal for balance, freshness, and secondary accent moments.
- Reserve bright or saturated color for emphasis, not full-page fill.
- Prefer subtle backgrounds and colored accents over heavy solid blocks.

## Interaction

- Buttons and links should look intentional and tactile, especially in hero and summary areas.
- Rounded pill actions are preferred for dashboard controls and top-level navigation.
- External actions should still feel consistent with internal navigation.
- Hover states should be gentle and polished, not flashy.

## Content behavior

- The dashboard should answer "what changed?" and "where is money going?" immediately.
- Summary pages should prioritize recent movement, top drivers, and next actions.
- Detailed tables and deep analysis can live one click away.

## Avoid

- Generic starter layouts with a plain heading and loose content underneath.
- Overuse of flat borders without contrast, depth, or hierarchy.
- Too many equally weighted panels on one screen.
- Bright rainbow palettes or unrelated accent colors.
- Dense copy that competes with the metrics.
